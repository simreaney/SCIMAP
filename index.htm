Identification of critical source areas.

Helping to spatially target land management to improve water qualty and reduse flood hazards

Degraded water quality and flood waters pose a significant threat to both people and ecology. Both of these pressure are from diffuse sources across the landscape that are concentrated into problems in rivers, lakes and the sea. SCIMAP provides a framework to consider where in the landscape these pressures are coming from and hence where mitigation actions would be most effective. SCIMAP works by identifying where there is a significant source of the pressure, related to attributes such as land cover, topographic position and ground slope gradient, and the connectivity, which is the ease with which material can make it to the channel. The SCIMAP framework considers four environmental pressures: 1. sediment, 2. nutrients (N and P), microbial pollution (FIOs such as e coli) and flood hazard generation.
